Who is shee that assends so high

 Next the heavenly Kinge,

Round about ahome angels flie

 And her prayses sing?

 

Who is shee, adorned with light,

 Makes the sunne her robe,

At whose feete the queene of night

 Layes her changing globe?

 

To that crowne direct thine eye.

 Which her heade attyres;

There thou mayest her name discrie

 Wrytt in starry fires.

 

This is shee in whose pure wombe

 Heaven’s Prince remained;

Therefore in no earthly tombe

 Cann shee be contained.

 

Heaven she was, which held that fire

 When the world tooke light,

And to heaven doth now aspire,

 Flames with fflames to unite.

 

She that did so clearly shyne

 When our day begunne,

See, how bright her beames decline

 Now she sytts with the sunne.

 

Sir John Beaumont (1583-1627)
