May is traditionally a month of devotion to Our Lady.  Throughout the month, at the end of every Mass, we shall recite the following prayer, which dates from the third century and is the oldest known prayer to Our Lady:-

 

We fly to thy Patronage, O holy Mother of God.  Despise not our prayers in our necessities but deliver us from all dangers, O glorious and blessed Virgin.

New guidelines for the celebration of Mass and other liturgical events have now been issued in the light of the government’s relaxation of rules that came into force last Monday. 

Some things remain unchanged – for example, holy water stoups must remain unfilled and there cannot be any physical contact at the sign of peace; face masks must still be worn, and social distance maintained. 

However, votive candles are now permitted, and the Prayer of the Faithful (Bidding Prayers) can once again be offered.  At a funeral, incense can now be used as well as holy water at the Final Commendation.

At the distribution of Holy Communion, the words ‘The Body of Christ’ can be used, with each communicant responding ‘Amen’, and it is permitted to receive Holy Communion on the tongue.  Those who wish to receive Holy Communion on the tongue rather than in the hand are asked to sanitise their hands, like everyone else, and (if possible and practicable) to receive after everyone who receives in the hand.
