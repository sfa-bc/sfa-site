 November is the month of the Holy Souls.  The names of those whom you wish to be remembered at Mass throughout the month can be placed in the casket before the altar; offerings can be placed in the box marked ‘Holy Souls’ by Our Lady’s altar.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Next Sunday is Remembrance Sunday and the Sunday morning Mass will be of Requiem for those who have lost their lives in armed conflict, especially the two World Wars of the twentieth century.  It will be, weather permitting, at the War Memorial Calvary in the graveyard.

The Calvary has been undergoing restoration, although at the time of writing the figure of Christ has not been re-attached.  It is hoped that this will happen soon and that the Calvary will stand for another century to mark the honour we owe to those who sacrificed their lives for our freedom.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------We need to recruit some more volunteers to help decorate the church throughout the year.  It is not an onerous task, but it is an important one; to provide flowers for the church and arrange them in the weeks of Ordinary Time.  You would be asked to do no more than half a dozen times during the course of a year.  If you would like to volunteer, please speak to Fr Sharp.

Peace be with those who have left us and gone to God.

May they be at peace.

May they be with God.

 

May they be with the living God.

May they be with the immortal God.

May they be in God’s hands.

 

May they sleep in peace.

May they live in peace.

May they be where the name of God is great.

 

May they be with the living God now and on the day of judgment.

May tey live with God.

May they live in eternal light.

 

May they live in the peace of the Lord.

May they live forever in peace.

With God in peace.
