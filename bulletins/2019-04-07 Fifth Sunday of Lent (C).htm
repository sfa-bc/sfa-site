As we enter the final phase of Lent and our thoughts turn more and more to the Passion of the Lord, we continue the custom of veiling all crucifixes and images, as a sign of mourning.  The crucifixes remain covered until after the Liturgy of Good Friday, the images until the Easter Vigil.  Votive candles are not burned before veiled images.

Next Sunday is Palm Sunday.  Weather permitting, the Sunday morning Mass will begin on the patch of grass outside the sacristy and church corridor, form where we shall process into church.

Evening Mass of the Lord’s Supper on Maundy Thursday will be at 7.30 pm, followed by Watching at the Altar of Repose (in the former Sisters’ Choir) until 9. 00 pm.

The liturgy of Good Friday will be at 3.00 pm, with Stations of the Cross and Veneration of the Relic of the True Cross at 7.30 pm.

The Easter Virgil, again weather permitting, will begin outside at 8.00 pm and lead into the Mass of the Resurrection.

The path to the Calvary in the old cemetery has been renewed and is looking splendid; that completes the schedule of works in that area following the restoration of the Calvary last year.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Decoration of the Church.  As previously announced, it is hoped to decorate the church early next year in preparation for the 150th anniversary of its opening on 4th October 1870.  Much thought has gone into a decorative scheme and final designs are being put together.  At the back of church are printouts of the designs for the sanctuary, together with some watercolour sketches which give an idea of colouring.

Fr Sharp is happy to explain the thinking behind the designs and the imagery at a parish meeting at 7.00 pm on Wednesday, 24th April.  In the meantime, please take a look and let him have your thoughts.
