--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------The organ was inspected this past week for the first time in nearly thirty years.  It probably dates from 1870, when the church was built, and originally the bellow mechanism was operated by the organist using foot paddles (rather like an harmonium); the foot pedals themselves were added later, possibly at the same time as the electric motor was installed. 

The resulting verdict was that it was a question of all or nothing.  The organ will continue to function without any work being done to it, but it will continue to deteriorate and at some point it will simply cease to function; the electric motor that drives the blower, for instance, dates from the 1920s!  The alternative is a complete re-furbishment; the inner workings would be taken away for repair and renewal - in effect, the organ would be re-built

If we are to decorate the church next year for the 150th anniversary of its opening, it seems sensible and proper to also restore the organ.  Inevitably, one job leads to another, but there is no point in doing only half a job.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- May is traditionally a month of devotion to our Blessed Lady.  Since the whole of the month this year falls within Eastertide, we shall sing the Regina Caeli at the end of every Mass as our May Devotion.  If you are unsure of the words, they can be found at number 616 in the hymn book.
