May is traditionally a month of devotion to our Blessed Lady.  Since the whole of the month this year falls within Eastertide, we shall sing the Regina Caeli at the end of every Mass as our May Devotion.  If you are unsure of the words, they can be found at number 616 in the hymn book.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The Diocesan Art and Architecture Committee met this past week and gave its approval to the proposed scheme of decoration of the church.  It is hoped that this will begin in January 2020, and we need to start thinking about how to raise the money to pay for it and the restoration of the organ, mentioned in last week’s bulletin.  We need to raise £120,000, of which £10,000 has already been pledged.  More on this in the coming weeks, but in the meantime everyone needs to start thinking how they can fundraise.

Rise, heart; thy Lord is risen.  Sing his praise

                                    Without delayes,

Who takes thee by the hand, that thou likewise

                                    With him mayst rise:

That, as his death calcined thee to dust,

His life may make thee gold, and much more just.

 

Awake, my lute, and struggle for thy part

                                     With all thy art.

The crosse taught all wood to resound to his name,

                                      Who bore the same.

His stretched sinews taught all strings, what key

Is best to celebrate this most high day.

 

From ‘Easter’ by George Herbert
