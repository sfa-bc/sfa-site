--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------2020 marks the 150th anniversary of the opening of the present church of St Francis of Assisi on 4th October 1870.  The archbishop will be coming to celebrate Mass with us on the day of the anniversary.  Meanwhile, we are embarking on a major scheme of decoration and restoration, including the re-building of the organ.  You may have noticed that already work has begun in the baptistery and this past week the organ was dismantled

From tomorrow, the church will be out of use and we shall move into the former Sisters’ Choir for all Masses and liturgical events; entrance will be by the side door of the church leading to the servers’ sacristy.  We shall be out of the church for six months or more, depending on progress. Confessions will be heard in the servers’ sacristy.

Today, we need to remove and put away all the Christmas decorations and remove from the church all furniture, statues, etc.  If you are able to come along at 2 pm and help with this task, it would be a great help.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------Tomorrow, Ordinary Time begins and the liturgical colour changes to green until Ash Wednesday, the beginning of Lent, on 26th February.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------The annual Parish Finance Meeting will be held after Mass on Sunday, 26th January.  This is an opportunity to look at our income and expenditure for 2019 and our projected budget for 2020.  Everyone is welcome to attend the meeting, which usually lasts about thirty minutes.
