‘Desirous I am, Peter, to tell you many things of this venerable father [St Benedict], but some of purpose I let pass, because I make haste to treat also of the acts of other holy men; yet I would not have you to be ignorant, but that the man of God amongst so many miracles for which he was so famous in the world, was also sufficiently learned in divinity, for he wrote a rule for his monks, both excellent for discretion, and also eloquent for the style.  Of whose life and conversation, if any be curious to know further, he may in the institution of that rule understand all his manner of life and discipline; for the holy man could not otherwise teach than he himself lived’.

 

From the Dialogues of St Gregory the Great
