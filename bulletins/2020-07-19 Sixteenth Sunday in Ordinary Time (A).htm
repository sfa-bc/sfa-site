Lord, when the sense of thy sweet grace

Sends up my soul to seek thy face

Thy blessed eyes breed such desire,

I dy in love’s delicious Fire.

O Love, I am thy sacrifice.

Be still triumphant, blessèd eyes.

Still shine on me, fair suns! That I

Still may behold, though still I dy.

 

Though still I dy, I live again;

Still longing so to be still slain,

So gainful is such losse of breath;

I dy even in desire of death.

Still live in me this loving strife

Of living Death and dying Life.

For while thou sweetly slayest me

Dead to my selfe, I live inThee.

 

Richard Crawshaw (1612-1649)
