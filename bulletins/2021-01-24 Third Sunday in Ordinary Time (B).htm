It was depressing even to think of it.  ‘How long?’ I asked the Lord, ‘How long will you be angry with me? For ever?  Can’t you forget my sins?’

You see, even if he forgot, I could not; and I could not believe he forgot, either.  I felt trapped still by my past failures.  So I cried to him: ‘Why not now, Lord – why not, at this very moment, put an end to this uncleanness?’

And the moment I said it, he answered.  I heard a voice, a far-away voice like a child calling from a distant house, repeating over and over again, ‘Pick up the book and read.  Pick up the book and read.’

So I picked up the book.  It was the letters of the apostle Paul.  I opened it, and read silently the first words on which my eyes fell: ‘Not parties, not getting drunk, not promiscuity, not quarrelling and envy – put off all those.  But put on the Lord Christ, and stop encouraging your lower nature.’

I need to read no more, for instantly – by the end of the sentence – a clear, bright light shone in my heart, driving out the darkness of my doubts.  It seemed so straightforward.  When I put off the old, that I had longed to lose, then I put on the new, the Lord Christ, whom I had longed to find.

 

St Augustine of Hippo (354-430 A.D.)
