May is traditionally a month of devotion to Our Lady.  Throughout the month, at the end of every Mass, we shall recite the following prayer, which dates from the third century and is the oldest known prayer to Our Lady:-

 

We fly to thy Patronage, O holy Mother of God.  Despise not our prayers in our necessities but deliver us from all dangers, O glorious and blessed Virgin Mary.

There is something repulsive about boasting of our own achievements.  But that should not stop us telling people about what God has done for us.  That is the difference – we do not pretend it is our doing that our lives are changed, or that we can do good deeds.  We make it absolutely clear that all our power for good comes from God.

And it does.  He is the source of whatever spiritual life we have, and whatever spiritual strength.  And he is also the source of whatever wisdom we have.  He gives us eyes that recognize the signs of the times.

There is one danger in this (other than pride, that is).  Because the Lord has been so generous, and given us forgiveness and strength, we may begin to take him for granted.  A healthy fear of God may be the best guard against presumption.  It will ensure that the wonderful sense of security God has given us does not lead to spiritual indifference.

Keep him as the guest of your soul.  Then there is no chance of the old Enemy creeping unawares and spoiling everything.

 

St Cyprian (200-258 A.D.)
