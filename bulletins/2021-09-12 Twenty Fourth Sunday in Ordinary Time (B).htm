TWENTY-FOURTH SUNDAY IN ORDINARY TIME (11th/12th  September 2021)

~ Fr Frank writes:  It is a great privilege to have been asked to care for the Parish of St Francis in addition to Blessed Robert Grissold church in Balsall Common.  It is because I have two Masses on a Sunday that I have had to change the time of the Sunday Mass at Baddesley Clinton to 1100.  I regret any inconvenience this has caused but, as I am sure you are aware, there is a shortage of priests and  many of us have to double up on our workload.  You have my assurance that I will do my best to serve both parishes fairly and to the best of my ability but, with the added workload, I am hoping for your practical support.  Please keep me in your prayers as you are in mine.

~ Weekday Masses  There will be a Mass at 9.00am on Thursdays at St Francis, Baddesley Clinton.  There will also be a Mass at BRG, Balsall Common at 10.00am on Tuesdays.  This arrangement assumes that the usual precautions and cleaning regime can be put in place.

~ A Summary of the Guidance from the Bishop’s Conference concerning Step 4 of the Government’s Covid-19 roadmap which comes into effect on Monday 19th July 2021.

At this stage it is important for us all to remember that although legislation has been relaxed, the pandemic is not yet over and there continues to be a duty of care for all who use our churches.  The guidance seeks to ensure there remains a cautious  approach that encourages the continuation of social distancing, mask wearing and cleaning regimes in order to help to minimise risk and provide comfort and confidence to our people.  Churches should continue to provide hand sanitiser at entrances and exits and face coverings are strongly recommended to be worn by those in church. General cleaning should be to a high standard with special attention to frequent touch points.  Buildings should be well ventilated and as long as the NHS Test, Track and Trace system is in operation, churches should continue to make available the means for people to sign in for acts of worship using the NHS app and the associated QR codes or by other  means of attendee identification.  In the light of the Bishop’s Guidance and the Government’s call for caution and with a care for the most vulnerable among us, I propose that we should continue in much the same way as we have been until the advice from the Archdiocese changes.  This will help to protect the vulnerable among us and it will also give confidence to people who are  hesitant about attending Mass. Please see below the restrictions which remain in place for the time being.  Thanks to all the volunteers who are making Mass possible. 

~ Masses at St Francis in Time of COVID-19 +  Attendees to wear a mask to protect themselves and others +  Remain 2 metres apart +  Sanitise hands on entering and exiting the Church +  Always follow the stewards’ instructions  + Holy Communion +  Masks to be worn to receive the Host + Please stay at home if...+  You are feeling ill or show any symptoms  +  You suspect you may be ill or have been exposed to Covid 19 +  You have a sick family member at home + You have come into contact with someone who is infected + Those attending will be asked to supply contact details for track and trace + Wrap up warm (if necessary) - doors and windows will be left open to increase ventilation wherever possible
