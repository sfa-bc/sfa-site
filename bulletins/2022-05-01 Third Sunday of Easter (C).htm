THIRD SUNDAY OF EASTER

 

The consequences of the Resurrection of our Lord Jesus Christ do not end with Easter Day.  For those for whom the power of the Resurrection was a first-hand experience, it affected the rest of their lives.  Today we remember that the Resurrection was a dynamic and transforming experience for the friends of Jesus and we pray that it may be so for us also.  For the Resurrection reveals an immense power working in peoples’ lives through time and space - a power which overcomes unbelief and doubt

- a power at work in all the baptised and made present whenever and wherever they gather for the Eucharist - that same power that raised Jesus from the dead - the mighty power of the resurrection which is in our midst today as we gather for Mass.

 

Thanks Once more I would like to thank you for the generosity you showed with your Easter Offering - it is very much appreciated, as is all the support I receive from you.  I'm looking forward to returning from my much needed Easter break and to being with you at Mass.

 

Prayer for the Ukraine  Let us continue to pray for the people of Ukraine, praying for a lasting peace and an end to war in that land, remembering also the Ukrainian Eparchy, for Bishop Kenneth Nowakowski, his clergy and people and the Ukrainian parishes in our own Archdiocese.
